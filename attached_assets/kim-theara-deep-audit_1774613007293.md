### Issue 1 — Visual Design Does Not Exist

The site renders as **unstyled browser-default HTML**. There is no:
- Typography system
- Color palette
- Spacing or layout grid
- Navigation bar
- Any visual element

**This is the most damaging issue** because Kim is a Product Designer.
A blank, unstyled page is the loudest possible statement that visual design
was not a priority — the exact opposite of what his profession demands.

**Fix:**
Rebuild with a deliberate design system. Minimum requirements:
- Custom font pairing (display font for headings, readable font for body)
- A primary brand color + neutral palette
- 8px grid spacing system
- Proper visual hierarchy (H1 → H2 → body → caption sizes)
- A sticky navigation bar

### Issue 3 — No Case Studies, Only Bullet Points

Four one-sentence bullets is not a portfolio. It is a list of employers.
Clients and hiring managers want to see **how you think**, not just **where you worked**.

**Fix — Use this case study template for each project:**

```
## Project: [Name]

### Overview
One paragraph: what it is, who it's for, your role.

### The Problem
What was broken or missing? What were users struggling with?
Include any research data or user quotes if available.

### My Role
Specifically what were you responsible for?
(Research / IA / Wireframing / UI / Prototyping / Testing / Handoff)

### Process
Step-by-step walkthrough with visuals:
1. Discovery & Research
2. Define & Synthesise
3. Ideate & Sketch
4. Wireframe
5. Design & Prototype
6. Test & Iterate
7. Final Handoff

### Key Design Decisions
Why did you choose X over Y?
What trade-offs did you make and why?

### Outcome
Metrics, user feedback, business impact.
Example: "Redesigned flow reduced task completion time by 40%"

### Reflection
What would you do differently?
What did you learn?
```

**Recommended first case studies:**
1. **SEA Observatory** — Most unique project, AI governance + UX is an extremely rare combo
2. **ABA Bank** — Scale and impact (hundreds of thousands of daily users)

### Issue 9 — No SEO or Social Metadata

Deep technical check shows:
- Page title is 80+ characters (too long for Google — max recommended is 60)
- No meta description
- No Open Graph tags (link previews on LinkedIn/Slack/Twitter will look broken)
- No canonical URL
- No structured data (JSON-LD for Person schema)
- No sitemap.xml

**Fix — Add to `<head>`:**

```html
<!-- SEO -->
<meta name="description" content="Kim Theara is a Product Designer and AI Researcher
based in Phnom Penh, Cambodia. UX Lead at ABA Bank. AI Governance at SEA Observatory." />
<title>Kim Theara — Product Designer & AI Researcher | Phnom Penh</title>

<!-- Open Graph (controls link previews everywhere) -->
<meta property="og:title" content="Kim Theara — Product Designer & AI Researcher" />
<meta property="og:description" content="UX Lead at ABA Bank. AI Governance at SEA Observatory across 11 ASEAN nations." />
<meta property="og:image" content="https://yourdomain.com/og-image.jpg" />
<meta property="og:url" content="https://yourdomain.com" />
<meta property="og:type" content="website" />

<!-- Twitter Card -->
<meta name="twitter:card" content="summary_large_image" />

<!-- Structured Data -->
<script type="application/ld+json">
{
```
